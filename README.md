Zomato Restaurant Market Analysis

A data analysis project that uses global Zomato restaurant data to recommend where the business should open its next restaurants — based on competition, ratings, cuisine preferences, and pricing. Built entirely in Microsoft Excel.


The Problem

Expanding a restaurant business into new markets is expensive and risky. Open in the wrong city and you fight saturated competition; misjudge local tastes or pricing and the restaurant fails.

The question this project answers:


Which countries and cities give Zomato the best chance of success for new restaurants, and how should those restaurants be positioned?



To answer it, the project works through a real, messy dataset to find markets that combine low competition with healthy customer ratings — the sweet spot for growth.


What I Did

1. Cleaned the data


Filled missing cuisine values using logical, location-based rules
Standardized inconsistent date formats for reliable analysis
Converted all foreign currencies to a single currency (INR) for fair comparison
Extracted Day / Month / Year columns to enable trend analysis


2. Explored the landscape (using pivot tables)


Counted restaurants per country and mapped their price-range distribution
Analyzed online delivery and table-booking availability and its effect on ratings


3. Identified the opportunity


Flagged 59 cities with fewer than 10 restaurants as low-competition growth zones
Shortlisted countries with light competition and average ratings between 3 and 4:
Australia, Brazil, Canada, Singapore, Sri Lanka


4. Analyzed the success factors


Cuisine analysis — found the highest-rated cuisines in each target country
Cost vs. rating correlation — tested whether price drives ratings (R² ≈ 0.03, i.e. it barely does)
Service gaps — found target markets largely lack online delivery and table booking


5. Visualized everything


Bar, line, pie, scatter, and histogram charts
A consolidated interactive Excel dashboard bringing all findings together

Conclusion

The analysis identifies a clear, data-backed path to expansion. Australia, Brazil, Canada, Singapore, and Sri Lanka — and specifically their low-competition cities — offer the best balance of light competition and solid customer ratings.

To succeed in these markets, new restaurants should:


Lead with high-rated local cuisines to win trust quickly
Price to each market's average spend (e.g. ~₹9,944 avg. in Singapore vs. ~₹682 in Sri Lanka), since pricing has almost no effect on ratings
Launch online delivery and table booking to capture an unmet convenience gap
Compete on food and service quality, the real drivers of customer ratings
Continuously monitor competitors and customer feedback to sustain growth


In short: the data points to where to expand, what to serve, and how to price and differentiate — turning a raw spreadsheet into an actionable expansion strategy.
